# Writing voice — felipetavares.dev

This file governs all prose in this repo: website copy, content files, docs, commit messages
that will be quoted publicly, UI microcopy, articles. It applies to EN and PT-BR equally.

It is adapted from the canonical personal skill at `~/.claude/skills/human-voice/SKILL.md` for
public consumers who cannot read that file. Same rules; this is the repo-scoped single source.

---

## 1. Banned lexicon

Never use reflexively (only when literally precise and no plainer word exists):

**Verbs:** delve, underscore, showcase, harness, foster, bolster, streamline, facilitate,
leverage, elevate, unlock, unleash, embark, navigate (metaphorically), illuminate, elucidate,
catalyze, galvanize, cultivate, augment, garner, empower, spearhead, revolutionize, resonate,
reimagine, encompass, underpin.

**Adjectives:** pivotal, crucial, vital, paramount, robust, seamless, comprehensive, holistic,
dynamic, innovative, cutting-edge, groundbreaking, game-changing, transformative, unprecedented,
meticulous, intricate, nuanced, multifaceted, compelling, remarkable, sophisticated, world-class,
state-of-the-art, agile, vibrant, thriving, burgeoning, nascent, quintessential, overarching,
poised, unwavering.

**Nouns:** tapestry, landscape (metaphorical), realm, mosaic, symphony, ecosystem (unless literal
biology/platform term), synergy, paradigm, journey (metaphorical), deep dive, learnings, thought
leader, cornerstone, testament, beacon, watershed moment, myriad, plethora, interplay.

**Copula-avoidance:** boasts, serves as, stands as, marks, represents, refers to — use plain
"is/has".

**Openers to avoid:** "In today's …", "In the ever-evolving …", "With the advent of", "It's
important to note", "It is worth noting", "When it comes to", "At its core", "Let's dive in".

**Closers to avoid:** "In conclusion", "In summary", "At the end of the day", "The future looks
bright", "Only time will tell", "As we move forward", any aphoristic universal truth.

**Vague authority:** "Experts argue", "Studies show", "Research suggests" without a named, dated
source.

PT-BR analogues: "mergulhar em", "panorama", "robusto", "crucial", "vibrante" — same ban.

## 2. Banned structures

- **The negation pivot** — "It's not just X, it's Y" / "Not X, but Y". The single most
  diagnostic AI construction. State the one claim.
- **Em-dash reflex** — only where a genuine interruption earns one; never more than one per
  paragraph.
- **Rule-of-three compulsion** — triple adjectives, triple clauses, "Fast. Simple. Effective."
  Use triplets rarely and deliberately.
- **Uniform rhythm** — vary sentence length deliberately. Some sentences four words. Some take
  their time.
- **Gerund-clause pseudo-analysis** — sentences opening "By leveraging…", "Highlighting the
  importance of…".
- **Synonym cycling** — repeat the clearest word; don't rotate substitutes.
- **Bullet inflation** — bullets only for genuinely enumerable content; never "**Bold label:**
  description" × 8 as fake prose.

---

## 3. Banned tones

- Sycophancy, acknowledgment loops, restating the heading as the first sentence under it.
- Significance inflation: "marks a pivotal moment", "is a testament to" for routine facts.
- Reaction-steering adverbs: "Interestingly," "Notably," "Importantly," "Undoubtedly."
- Hedge-stacking ("could potentially", "may eventually perhaps"); false balance.
- Announced-not-earned emotion ("What fascinated me most…") — write the specific thing.
- Rhetorical-question stalls ("So why does this matter?").
- Empty future-prophecy closers ("poised to define the next chapter").

---

## 4. Banned formatting

Emoji as headers/bullets. Bold on every other phrase. Reflexive "Key takeaways"/TL;DR boxes.
Leftover placeholders.

---

## 5. Positive rules

1. **Specificity first.** Names, numbers, dates. "Cut build time from 9 min to 90 s" beats
   "significantly improved performance".
2. **First-person stakes.** What I did, chose, got wrong — not what "one might consider".
3. **Asymmetric emphasis.** Three paragraphs on the one thing that matters; one line for the
   rest.
4. **Commit.** One recommendation, defended. "It depends" only with the actual dependency named.
5. **Reuse the right word** instead of elegant variation.
6. **Fragments allowed.** Starting with And/But allowed. Read it aloud — that is the test.
7. **Cut the frame, keep the claim.** Delete throat-clearing; start where the point starts.
8. **Structure follows argument.** If two paragraphs can swap without damage, rebuild it.
9. **Density check.** If a paragraph survives a 50% cut, make the cut.
10. **Voice quirks stay.** A dry aside or an odd-but-precise phrase is worth more than uniform
    polish.

## Self-check before publishing

- Zero section-1 lexicon hits (or each survivor individually justified).
- Zero negation pivots; at most one em-dash per ~150 words; no tricolon clusters.
- At least one concrete number or name per ~150 words of claims.
- Sentence-length variance visible; at least one short sentence per section.
- Ends on substance, not a bow.
